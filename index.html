StrongFirst Wisdom

Key lessons from the S&S community & forum

Common Mistake #1

Moving too fast through the programme. S&S is a practice, not a workout. Don't chase the time standard every session — only test every 2 weeks. Train below the anaerobic threshold (talk test) most days.

Common Mistake #2

Gripping the bell too tightly on swings. Death grip kills endurance and power. Grip firm but not white-knuckle. Let the bell do the work on the float.

Common Mistake #3

Skipping light days. Pavel Macek: "The 'light day' option in S&S is one of the secrets of the program. Many practitioners forget it, but it is one of the keys to success at heavier weights."

Common Mistake #4

Rushing to move up in weight before owning the current bell. "Own the weight" means every rep is crisp, powerful, and you pass the talk test between sets. If you can't, you're not ready.

The Secret

S&S is a recharge, not a workout. "The purpose of a training session is to store energy in the body rather than exhaust it." — Ivan Ivanov, Bulgarian gymnastics coach

Forum Gold

Grip work (Captains of Crush grippers, farmer's carries) made a massive difference for people struggling with 32kg+. Don't skip the warm-up prying goblet squats even when you're advanced — Pavel says keep those forever.

When You're Stuck

Brett Jones (StrongFirst Director): If you stall, check three things — are you practising too infrequently? Are you resting enough between sets? Are you skipping light days? Almost everyone needs to back off and go slower than they think.

Night Shift Workers

Forum members on shift patterns report the biggest gains come from consistency over intensity. 100 swings with a moderate bell 5x/week beats 200 swings with a heavy bell 2x/week. Your CNS is already taxed — meet it where it is.

Breath Timing

10 swings, 5 breaths. Slow your breathing down between sets — this IS the rest. Shinkokyuu (deep abdominal breathing) while standing with a deadpan face. The calmer your breath, the longer your rest, the more powerful your next set.

Shadow Swings

The eccentric (downswing) is as important as the concentric. Let the bell pull you into a fast, powerful hike. F=ma works in both directions. Don't fight the bell down — ride it.
